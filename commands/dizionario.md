# Dizionario — Italian for Developers

## /al-dente
**Italian:** "Al dente" (literally: "to the tooth")
**Meaning:** Pasta cooked so it's firm when you bite it — not mushy, not raw. The perfect texture.
**In code:** The balance between over-engineering and under-engineering.
**Learn more:**

## /arrangiati
**Italian:** "L'arte di arrangiarsi" (the art of making do)
**Meaning:** A deeply Italian skill — the ability to improvise solutions with whatever you have. Born from generations of resourcefulness, it's a point of cultural pride.
**In code:** Solving problems with existing dependencies. No new packages.
**Learn more:**
- [L'arte di arrangiarsi, one of Italy's best skills](https://italoamericano.org/sapersi-arrangiare/)
- [Arrangiarsi: the Italian art of making do](https://www.italymagazine.com/featured-story/arrangiarsi-italian-art-making-do)

## /bella-figura
**Italian:** "Fare bella figura" (to make a good figure/impression)
**Meaning:** Italians care deeply about presentation — not just appearance, but showing respect through how you present yourself and your work. It's about dignity, not vanity.
**In code:** Clean formatting, proper naming, removing dead code. Respecting the reader.
**Learn more:**
- [Fare bella figura: to make a good impression](https://www.parlaitaliano.co.uk/fare-bella-figura/)
- [The Italian concept of Bella Figura](https://www.wantedinrome.com/news/the-italian-concept-of-bella-figura.html)

## /coffee-break
**Italian:** "La pausa caffe" (the coffee pause)
**Meaning:** In Italy, the coffee break is sacred. It's not about caffeine — it's about stepping away, resetting, and letting your subconscious work. The best ideas happen at the bar, not at the desk.
**In code:** When you're stuck, stop pushing. Think laterally.
**Learn more:**
- [The Italian coffee break: origins and why it's important](https://pontevecchiosrl.it/en/coffee-break-origins-and-why-it-is-done/)
- [Italian coffee culture explained](https://www.lacucinaitaliana.com/italian-food/italian-dishes/italian-coffee-culture)

## /ma-che-vuoi
**Italian:** "Ma che vuoi?" (But what do you want?)
**Meaning:** The iconic Italian gesture — fingers pinched together, hand raised. It expresses bewilderment, frustration, or a demand for explanation. The emoji version is well known globally.
**In code:** Demanding that confusing code explain itself.
**Learn more:**
- [The pinched fingers emoji and its Italian origins](https://emojipedia.org/pinched-fingers)
- [Italian hand gestures explained](https://learnamo.com/en/63-famous-italian-gestures/)

## /nonna-review
**Italian:** "La nonna" (grandmother)
**Meaning:** The Italian grandmother is a cultural icon — she feeds everyone, she speaks her mind, she worries about whether you're eating enough and dressing warmly. Her advice comes from love and decades of experience.
**In code:** A code review that's warm but honest. Starts with praise, then tells you what needs fixing.
**Cultural note:** In Italian families, nonna's kitchen is the real headquarters. Her judgment is final. If nonna says your code needs tests, you write tests.
**Learn more:**
- [The role of the grandmother in Italian culture](https://www.italymagazine.com/featured-story/nonna-italian-grandmother)
- [Italian proverbs about wisdom](https://www.thinkinitalian.com/italian-proverbs/)

## Bonus: Italian Proverbs for Developers

- **"Chi dorme non piglia pesci"** — Who sleeps doesn't catch fish. (You snooze, you lose.)
- **"Chi va piano va sano e va lontano"** — Who goes slowly goes safely and goes far. (Slow and steady wins the race.)
- **"Sbagliando s'impara"** — By making mistakes, one learns. (Debugging is learning.)
- **"Ogni scarrafone e bello a mamma soja"** (Neapolitan) — Every bug is beautiful to its mother. (You'll always be blind to your own code's flaws. Get a review.)
